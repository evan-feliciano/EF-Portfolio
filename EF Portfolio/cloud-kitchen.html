Project Overview

Since the beginning of time, sharing meals, cooking, and eating good food have brought people together in a unique way. Since then, different advances like fire, electricity, and the internet have continually revolutionized the way we approach eating and cooking, and also how we share those experiences with others. Cloud Kitchen is bringing together those old traditions with the newest advances in cloud technology to create a modern platform for users to experience food in new ways. Cloud Kitchen is a platform that allows users to create, collaborate and explore different types of food content, like recipes, diets, meal-plans, cooking techniques, and much more. Users can personalize their experience on the platform, as well as share their favorite content with friends, family, and social media.

Research and Discovery

The first step of every successful design, always begins with research. I conducted to a survey to collect data on user’s behavior and needs regarding cloud technology, cooking and eating, and social habits, and to ensure that users were interested in using food-first product. Analyzing this data allowed me to draw out a few key takeaways: First, users are used to a high level of personalization and are very frustrated with lack of organizational features from existing products. Second, over 87% of users find the majority of their recipes online, but have no organized method of storing, saving, and sorting these recipes. Lastly, 90% users expressed interest in exploring, downloading and sharing other people’s recipes and food content in addition to being able to create their own.

With the information from this survey, I built 2 user personas that detailed what the target audiences would look like. The first was a younger group, interested in trying new foods and travelling to places, and posting about everything along the way. The second group was a bit older, less used to cloud tech, but more affluent and much used to cooking most meals at home.

Competitive Analysis

With other huge competitors in the cloud space, the food focus would be a sure way to establish our own postition amongst some of the most well-established players. With that being said, there’s still a lot we can takeaway from seeing what others are already doing well and where they could improve. Google Drive is probably the most common cloud tool, and offers a ton of awesome creative tools for individuals and bootstrapped businesses to choose from. However, their organization tools are very minimal, and often leave users with a cluttered and confusing interface. iCloud plays off their iOS theme which gives it a creative feel and keeps things very organized, unfortunately, most of these tools are kept locked behind a paywall. Dropbox feels the most formal and secure with it’s simple design. And while organizational tools could be improved upon, they offer users the choice to use their native machine’s storage method, which eases most of the burden when it comes to clean storage and easy access.

Branding

After fleshing out the blueprints to platform, getting feedback from the users, and adjusting the design, the next step was to add some life and branding into the wireframes. I love the name Cloud Kitchen because it’s very simple and speaks to exactly what the functionality of the platform is.

For typography I chose Poppins for the main font, and Fira Sans for the secondy font. Both are modern, but Poppins is bit more stylish and and inspiring for logos and call-to-actions, while Fira Sans is safe and easy to read in larger bodies of text. The logo itself, reiterates the simplicity of the name as utensils over a cloud, with other color and text versions for different backgrounds and devices.

For colors, I went with a bold, pantone red as the main brand color because a majority of the content would end up being user generated, and the red still cuts through well. I also added some blacks, grays, and whites, as well as comforting blue to contrast when red might feel a little invasive.

Final Design
